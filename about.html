About Me

  • Student
  • Hobby programmer
  • Underpaid wage-worker

I've been programming since 2009 now. I started in vb.net and Visual Studio 2008. A few years later I moved to C# and rarely do I look back to vb.net.

I also have familiarity with rust, java, c++, go, (of course) python, and to an extent, ruby.

I enjoy compiler/type/language theory and am currently working on my own COW implementation.

I'm fanatical about writing clean, maintainable code.

I don't really like front-end work, I prefer working on gritty stuff like networking, threading, API implementation.

My team won second place in the University of West Florida's codefest in 2019 for a mobile app which was designed to reduce driving anxiety. My role was thankfully middle-ware (read the bit about my preferences in the previous line) - I designed and implemented an API that allowed communication from our server to our front-end via a REST api.

Finally and fairly unorignally, I enjoy tinkering with Arduinos and RaspberryPis.
